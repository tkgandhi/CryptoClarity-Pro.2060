DLTs (Distributed Ledger Technologies)

Digital networks that let multiple parties record/share information simultaneously without a central authority.

What is a Distributed Ledger?

A distributed ledger is a digital record of transactions that is shared across many computers, called nodes. Each node stores a copy of the ledger, so no single organization controls the data. This makes the system more reliable and transparent.


How Do DLTs Record and Share Transactions?

When a transaction occurs, it is broadcast to all nodes in the network. The nodes verify the transaction and update their copies of the ledger once agreement is reached. This keeps the data synchronized across the network.


What Is Consensus and Why Is It Important?

Consensus is the process nodes use to agree on which transactions are valid. It prevents fraud and ensures all participants record the same information. Different DLTs use methods like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake.


Why Are Distributed Ledgers Secure?

DLTs use cryptography and decentralization to protect data. Because the ledger is shared across many nodes, altering a record would require changing most copies at the same time. This makes tampering extremely difficult.


Are All Distributed Ledgers Blockchains?

Not all distributed ledgers are blockchains. While blockchains store data in linked blocks, some DLTs use other structures, such as Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs), to improve speed and scalability.
