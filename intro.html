An Introduction to Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrency is changing how people think about money, ownership, and digital systems.


What is Cryptocurrency?

  • Cryptocurrency is digital money that exists only online.
  • It allows people to send value directly without a middleman.
  • Most cryptocurrencies are global and operate 24/7.

Decentralization

  • Most cryptocurrencies are not controlled by banks or governments.
  • They rely on thousands of computers across the world.
  • This reduces central points of failure.

Blockchain Technology

  • A blockchain is a type of DLT and is a public record of transactions.
  • Each block is linked to the one before it.
  • Once added, data is extremely difficult to change.

Transparency

  • Transactions can be viewed by anyone on the blockchain.
  • Wallet addresses are visible, not personal identities.
  • This creates trust without needing a central authority.

Security & Cryptography

  • Cryptography protects transactions and wallets.
  • Private keys prove ownership of funds.
  • Losing a private key usually means losing access permanently.

Volatility

  • Cryptocurrency prices can change quickly.
  • Volatility is influenced by news, adoption, and demand.
  • This creates both risk and opportunity.

Wallets

  • Crypto is stored in digital wallets, not bank accounts.
  • Hot wallets stay connected to the internet.
  • Cold wallets stay offline for extra security.

Use Cases Beyond Money

  • Crypto powers NFTs, DeFi, and blockchain-based games.
  • Smart contracts automate agreements.
  • Many projects aim to replace traditional services.

Regulation & the Future

  • Crypto laws differ from country to country.
  • The technology is still evolving.
  • Global adoption continues to grow.
